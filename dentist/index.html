What's inside the kit

19 pages, designed to be used together. Print once, use for every appointment.

Visual Story

8 illustrated panels walking through the real steps — the waiting room, the special chair that leans back, the bright light, the tool that counts teeth, the tooth polish, the fluoride, rinsing, and going home.

Parent Scripts

5 ready-to-say phrases for the moments that are hardest to navigate — the waiting room, when the chair leans back, the tooth counting, the polish smell or taste, and the end of the visit.

Step Schedule Cards

12 illustrated cards showing each step of the dentist visit. Take them in your bag. Pull them out in the waiting room so your child can follow along.

Sensory Expectations

What your child will see (the bright light), hear (the suction tool), feel (the polish, the chair), and taste (the fluoride) — framed as reassuring preparation, not a list of things to worry about.

Brave Choices

8 coping options including squeezing a toy, counting ceiling tiles, and breathing through the nose — things your child can practise at home before they sit in the chair.

Custom Cards + More

Blank cards to fill in details specific to your child's appointment, a comfort plan, debrief questions for after, and parent co-regulation tips.

Who this kit is for

This kit is for parents who have taken a child to the dentist and wished they had something to say on the way there.

It works particularly well for children who need to know exactly what to expect before they sit in an unfamiliar chair. That includes children who are naturally cautious, children with dental anxiety, and children who find sensory experiences hard. The predictability built into this kit is the same thing that makes it useful for sensory-sensitive and neurodivergent children.

You don't need to have a child with diagnosed anxiety for this to help. Most children under 8 benefit from knowing what's coming. This kit gives you a clear, warm way to tell them.

Common questions

When should I use this kit?

Read the visual story together 1–3 nights before the appointment. Repeat it 2–3 times if your child finds the dentist particularly worrying. The step cards can go in your bag and come out in the waiting room.

Is this kit helpful for children who need extra preparation?

Yes. The kit was designed with predictability as the core principle — exactly what children who need to know what to expect benefit from most. The visual story walks through the real dentist steps in clear, honest language, including what they will hear from the suction tool and feel during the polish. It works well for sensory-sensitive and neurodivergent children. Educational support, not medical advice.

What if my child needs a filling or something not in the kit?

The kit covers the standard steps of a paediatric check-up. If your child needs additional treatment, tell them calmly what will be different — the blank custom cards let you add those steps in your own words. Educational support, not medical advice.
