Why reading it together the night before actually helps

Each kit is built around three ideas that make a real difference — no clinical jargon, just things that work at your kitchen table.

When children have seen the steps before, the experience feels familiar instead of unknown. Familiarity is the fastest path to calm.

They get to make choices

Every kit includes "you choose" moments — a comfort item to bring, a breathing option to try. Small choices build big confidence.

You read it together

These kits are designed for your kitchen table, not a waiting room. Reading together is half the work — your calm helps them feel safe.

Questions parents ask

Everything you need to know before you print.

How much does a kit cost?

Each kit is $4.99. You get a 19-page PDF download with instant access — no subscription, no recurring fees. Print as many times as you need for your family.

Can I print it more than once?

Yes — print as many copies as your family needs. The file is yours to keep. Many parents print a fresh copy each time an appointment comes around.

What age is this for?

The kits are designed for children ages 2–8. The visual story uses simple words and illustrated panels so even pre-readers can follow along with a parent. Older children in this range often love being able to read parts themselves.

Do I need to read it with my child?

Yes — reading together is half the work. The kits are designed for your kitchen table, 1–3 nights before the appointment. Your calm presence while reading is what makes the difference. The Parent Scripts page gives you exact things to say if you're not sure what words to use.

What file format is it? What do I need to print it?

Each kit is a PDF download. You'll get it instantly after purchase on Etsy. Print on standard US Letter paper — no special paper, laminator, or supplies needed. A basic home printer works perfectly.

What if my child doesn't seem interested or ready?

That's very normal. Try starting with just the visual story pages — no pressure to cover the whole kit in one sitting. Some children need two or three reads before the appointment to feel ready. The kit includes a When Plans Change page specifically for days when things don't go as hoped. Educational support, not medical advice.
