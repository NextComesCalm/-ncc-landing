Who this kit is for

This kit is for parents who find bedtime a daily battle — the delay, the one more, the coming back out again.

It works particularly well for children who find separation hard, children who need predictability to settle, and children who use bedtime as a bid for more connection — the 'one more thing' pattern. The consistent sequence and visual chart give children what they actually need: a predictable, known ending to the day.

You don't need to have a child with diagnosed anxiety for this to help. Bedtime is hard for most children under 8 — and a clear, consistent routine is the thing that changes it.

Common questions

How long should a bedtime routine take?

30–45 minutes works well for most children ages 2–8. Shorter than 20 minutes doesn't allow enough wind-down time. The goal is unhurried and purposeful — every step has a place. Educational support, not medical advice.

What if my child keeps getting out of bed after lights out?

The kit includes a 'hall pass' system: give your child one or two physical tokens at bedtime they can exchange for a legitimate request. Once the tokens are gone, no more exchanges. This gives children an outlet while capping the unlimited 'one more.' Educational support, not medical advice.

Is this helpful for children who find separation or transitions hard?

Yes — bedtime involves several things children find genuinely difficult: separating from the parent, ending the day's stimulation, and accepting the quiet. For children who find transitions and separation harder than most, a visual routine reduces the resistance significantly because nothing is a surprise. Educational support, not medical advice.
