What's inside the kit

19 pages, designed to make every ending predictable — for every activity, every day. Print once, use across all transitions.

Visual Story

8 illustrated panels: doing an activity at the park, parent gives a five-minute warning, second warning at two minutes, one last thing to finish, saying goodbye to the activity, moving to what's next, and settling into the new thing.

Parent Scripts

5 ready-to-say phrases: the five-minute warning, the two-minute warning, the goodbye to the activity, for when they resist, and for when they've successfully made it through.

Step Schedule Cards

12 illustrated cards for common daily transitions — park, play, dinner, bath, screen, leaving a friend's house. The same warning-and-signal sequence, ready for any activity.

Consistent Transition Signal

Explains how to build and use a recurring signal — a timer, a phrase, a song — that tells a child's brain a transition is coming. Includes guidance on how to introduce it and make it stick.

Brave Choices

The 'one more thing' finish, the goodbye wave to the activity, breathing through the first moment of resistance, and always knowing what the next step is before the transition starts.

Custom Cards + More

Blank cards for family-specific transitions, a comfort plan, and debrief questions to help your child build language for how transitions feel.

How to use it

1

Print at home

Download the PDF and print on US Letter paper. No special paper or printer required.

2

Read together first

Read the visual story with your child before a known hard transition. Talk about the signal, the warnings, and what comes next. Practise the goodbye wave together.

3

Use the same sequence

Use the same warning, the same signal, the same sequence — every time. Consistency is the tool. After a few weeks, the signal itself becomes calming.

Who this kit is for

This kit is for parents whose child struggles with every transition every day — leaving the park, stopping play, switching from one activity to the next.

It works particularly well for children who have difficulty with demand avoidance, children who become deeply absorbed in activities and find ending them genuinely hard, and children for whom any unexpected change feels like a lot. The consistent signal and warning sequence gives a child's brain time to prepare — rather than arriving at the ending without warning.

You don't need to have a child with a diagnosis for this to help. Transitions are hard for most children under 8. Predictability is what changes them.

Common questions

What if I give warnings and my child still falls apart?

Warnings reduce the intensity and duration of transition difficulties — they don't eliminate them, especially early on. Keep using the warning system consistently. Over several weeks most children's transitions improve significantly. Educational support, not medical advice.

Is it okay to physically help a child through a transition if they won't come?

Yes — calmly and without anger, physically helping a child through a transition is appropriate and sometimes necessary. 'I'm going to help your body get to the car' said matter-of-factly is very different from forcing a child in frustration. Educational support, not medical advice.

Is this helpful for children who find transitions harder than most?

Yes — this kit was designed specifically for children whose transition difficulties are consistent and daily, not occasional. For children who find any ending hard, the predictable warning-signal-next-step sequence builds the brain's 'transition mode' over time. Educational support, not medical advice.
