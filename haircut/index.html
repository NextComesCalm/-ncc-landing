What's inside the kit

19 pages, designed to be used together. Print once, use for every appointment.

Visual Story

8 illustrated panels: walking in, saying hello to the haircutter, sitting in the special chair, the cape going on, hearing what the scissors sound like, the comb and scissors, seeing the finished hair, going home.

Parent Scripts

5 ready phrases: describing the sounds before you go, for when the cape goes on, if the scissors feel too close, for the neck trim, and for the end.

Step Schedule Cards

12 illustrated cards to bring along to the appointment. Pull them out so your child can follow each step as it happens.

Sensory Expectations

What to see (mirrors, the cape), hear (scissors snipping, clippers buzzing), and feel (the comb, water spray, scissors near ears) — framed as preparation, not fear.

Brave Choices

8 coping options including holding something, watching in the mirror, counting snips, and breathing — small choices that give your child a sense of control.

Custom Cards + More

Blank cards to add details specific to your child's hairdresser, a comfort plan, debrief questions for after, and parent co-regulation tips.

Who this kit is for

This kit is for parents who have tried to take a child for a haircut and had it go sideways — and would like the next one to go differently.

It works particularly well for children who find sensory experiences hard — the sounds of scissors, the feeling of the cape, someone touching their head. That includes children who are naturally cautious and children who feel physical sensations more intensely than other kids.

You don't need a child with diagnosed anxiety for this to help. Most children under 8 benefit from knowing what's coming. This kit gives you a clear, warm way to walk them through it — at home, before you go.

Common questions

When should I use this kit?

Read the visual story together 1–3 nights before the appointment. Repeat it 2–3 times if your child finds the haircut particularly worrying. The step cards can go in your bag and come out in the waiting area.

What if my child needs a clippers trim — is that covered?

Yes. The visual story covers both scissors and clippers and describes what each sounds and feels like. The sensory expectations page specifically covers the buzzing sound and the feeling near the ears and neck. Educational support, not medical advice.

Is this kit helpful for children with sensory sensitivities?

Yes — the kit was specifically designed to walk through the physical sensations a child will experience: the sounds of scissors and clippers, the feeling of the cape, the comb. Many parents of sensory-sensitive and neurodivergent children find this page the most useful part of the kit. Educational support, not medical advice.
